Treatment Planning Note

Laser hair reduction works best as a planned course of treatments. Your practitioner will advise on the recommended number of sessions, treatment intervals, preparation, and aftercare based on your skin and hair profile.

UNDERSTANDING HAIR GROWTH CYCLES

Laser hair reduction works in stages because hair grows in cycles, not all at once. At any given time, only a portion of hair follicles can be effectively treated.

Hair growth occurs in three phases:

Active Growth Phase

This is when the hair is actively growing and connected to the follicle. Laser hair reduction is most effective during this phase.

Transition Phase

Hair stops growing and begins to detach from the follicle. Laser effectiveness is reduced at this stage.

Resting Phase

Hair is inactive and will eventually shed. Laser cannot effectively target hair in this phase.

Because not all hairs are in the active growth phase at the same time, multiple treatment sessions are required to achieve optimal hair reduction.

RECOMMENDED TREATMENT INTERVALS

To align with natural hair growth cycles, sessions are scheduled as follows:

	•	Facial areas: every 4 weeks

	•	Body areas: every 6 to 8 weeks

Following these intervals allows new hairs to enter the active growth phase before each session, improving treatment effectiveness.

WHAT TO EXPECT OVER TIME

With consistent treatments:

	•	Hair grows back slower and finer

	•	Hair density gradually reduces

	•	Long-term hair reduction becomes visible

Laser hair reduction is a progressive treatment, and best results are achieved by completing the recommended course.
